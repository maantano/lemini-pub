## Design Context

### Users
Korean office workers, founders, and general users ask practical legal questions when they need a fast starting point, usually in a mildly stressful situation. They want a clear answer, the exact law article behind it, and enough confidence to decide whether they should read further or seek professional legal advice.

### Brand Personality
Trustworthy, calm, precise. The interface should feel like a well-edited public service publication rather than a flashy AI demo or a heavy corporate law-firm portal.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-first editorial product with warm paper neutrals, deep ink tones, and restrained accent colors. The product should feel evidence-first, modern, and quietly premium, while avoiding purple gradients, glassy AI tropes, or courtroom cliches.

### Design Principles
1. Evidence appears before decoration.
2. Complex legal structure should become scannable through typography and spacing.
3. The product should lower anxiety with calm copy, clear affordances, and predictable hierarchy.
4. Retrieval confidence and cost-awareness should be visible in operational surfaces.
5. Mobile and web should share the same calm editorial tone, not two unrelated visual systems.
